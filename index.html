Article Comprehension — Inference Questions
Read the article, then answer the questions using inference.
Remote working has become a permanent feature of modern employment. While many employees appreciate the flexibility and the elimination of daily commutes, managers have raised concerns about maintaining team cohesion and monitoring productivity. Research suggests that workers who have the option to work remotely at least two days per week report higher job satisfaction, yet paradoxically, they also report feeling more isolated than their office-based colleagues. Companies are now investing in digital collaboration tools and scheduling regular in-person "anchor days" to address both productivity and wellbeing concerns.
Q1What concern do managers have about remote working?
  • A Higher salaries
  • B Team cohesion and productivity
  • C Too many employees commuting
  • D Lack of digital tools
Q2What is the "paradox" mentioned in the article?

  • B Remote workers are more satisfied but feel more isolated
  • C Managers support remote work but employees don't
  • D Companies save money but spend more on tools
Q3What are "anchor days" most likely designed to do?
  • A Let employees work fully from home
  • B Reduce company costs
  • C Bring teams together in person regularly
  • D Replace digital collaboration tools
Q4Based on the article, which statement is most accurate?
  • A Remote working has no downsides
  • B Most employees prefer full office work
  • C Hybrid working has both benefits and challenges
  • D Companies are removing digital tools
Score—
Answer Key
Q1B — Team cohesion & productivity
Q2B — More satisfied but isolated
Q3C — Bring teams together
Q4C — Both benefits & challenges
